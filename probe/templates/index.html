Metric Descriptions

Internet Quality Score

Composite score from 0–100 based on packet loss, latency, jitter, and DNS response time. Higher is better; values below ~80 may indicate degraded experience.

Packet Loss

Percentage of ICMP packets that never reach their destination. Healthy connections are usually near 0%. Spikes may indicate congestion, cabling issues, or ISP problems.

Latency to Anchors

Round-trip time to your router / gateway and several public sites. Persistent high latency affects games, VoIP, and other real-time applications.

Jitter

How much latency varies between packets. High jitter is bad for interactive traffic even when average latency looks fine.

DNS Response Time

Time for DNS servers to resolve the test domain. High values may cause pages to feel "slow to start" even if raw bandwidth is good.
